

I selected the Great Depression as my historical event, specifically the role played by income inequality in exacerbating the economic crisis of the time.

The Great Depression was an economic crisis that impacted millions of lives around the world, not just in the United States, and resulted in unemployment, poverty, and the collapse of economic structures for the better part of a decade. It reshaped global economic policy in a radical way and led to much larger government intervention in the economy, with implications that persist to this day.

**Secondary Sources**

The two secondary sources I’m using for my research are *The Great Crash, 1929* by John Kenneth Galbraith and *Golden Fetters: The Gold Standard and the Great Depression, 1919-1939* by Barry Eichengreen. I located these sources on my bookshelf in the case of *The Great Crash*, and via a Google search for *Golden Fetters*.

*The Great Crash* focuses on the income disparity between the rich and poor in the late 1910s and early 1920s, leading up to the crash of the stock market. It provides analysis of the economy in the time period leading to the crash.

My other source, *Golden Fetters*, was chosen to provide a wider scope of context, covering the relationship between the domestic economic situation and the international economic situation, and the contributions of the concentration of wealth and flow of gold.

Both sources relate to my original research topic—the influence of income inequality—but from different points of view. Galbraith’s book gives a more United States or domestic-centered approach on the topic, while Eichengreen’s book provides a global or international scope to the topic. I consider both to be credible sources with good arguments that will help me investigate my event.

**Primary Sources**

For primary sources, I’ve chosen to use FDR’s fireside chats because this will show a direct viewpoint, as well as the viewpoint of the government itself, on the crisis and the planned response. I’m also looking into using newspapers from the era to provide a social perspective from the working man’s media consumption point of view.

**Research Question**

The research question I will use moving forward is: How did income inequality leading to the Great Depression influence this event and future America?

This question will allow me to delve deeper into the social and economic factors that contributed to the Great Depression and will allow me to investigate the impacts and lasting consequences of this time period.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
