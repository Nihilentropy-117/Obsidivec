

I suppose the question is tied between whether there is a higher power of some sort organizing everything or whether everything is just mathematically connected.

On a very basic level, I believe the saying goes: a butterfly flapping its wings in Trinidad causes a typhoon in Mumbai, or something like that. It’s the basic principles of chaos theory and whatnot—the smallest changes in the initial boundary conditions of a closed system can drastically affect the outcomes, given a complex system.

So there’s probably the world where every single action, every single thought, breath, falling tree—every single thing that happens anywhere, all the time, ever—has small, imperceptible effects on every other thing, thought, and falling tree that has or will ever happen.

But I guess depending on where these boundaries are drawn, perhaps these effects and causes are not always quite so imperceptible. There almost must be an upper and lower bound as to what we can sense, what we can pick up on. Especially if we take into consideration the fact that we have been living for hundreds of thousands of years evolved specifically in a world in which this mathematical interconnectivity has always existed.

As a species, we live in a world rich with refracted electromagnetic energy and photons in the reasonable light spectrum, so we evolved eyes. We evolved in a world with vibrations in the air—waves of compression—so we evolved ears. We evolved in a world where there is a large amount of particulate floating around randomly, so we evolved noses.

Would it really be that surprising that since we evolved in a world that contains both butterflies and typhoons and a mathematical connection between them, we might have evolved some sort of way of picking that up on a subconscious level?

This would explain a number of other constructs, such as luck. Although it would have the rather unusual effect of implying that we could breed for the thing we call luck. Although it wouldn’t be luck, of course. It would just be an innate ability to go with the flow, as it were. An innate ability to pick up what we perceive of the world around us using our existing senses and determine at a subconscious level the most likely probabilities of events.

We already do this all the time, every day. So is it really so absurd to think that perhaps some of us have a subconsciously heightened sense of these probabilities?

As far as I’m concerned, there is definitely something out there in the mathematical void between the atoms. I don’t know if it’s an intelligent, higher form of being. It may just be a series of mathematical constructs linking things together. It may be a god. It may be *the* God.

There’s really no way of telling from our earthly point of view. And to be quite honest, it doesn’t really matter. All that matters is that we do what we’re going to do.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
