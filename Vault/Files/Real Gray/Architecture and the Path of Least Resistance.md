In accordance with the teachings of the narrative, the correct way to initiate action to cause a thing to happen is not to do the thing directly, or even to directly cause the thing to happen.

Under the teachings of the narrative, the correct way is to create an environment in which the happening of the thing is the most direct course of action the environment will naturally take. This allows for variations to occur. This allows for entropy and chaos to take their effect. This allows for life to have a say in the action.

You don’t roll a ball down a hill; you adjust the hill so the ball is likely to roll on its own.

That might be a bad example. Physics doesn’t really follow that, I guess. It’s really more of a social commentary, though I would definitely like to see if there’s any mathematical parallel to this.

But it definitely works with people. You can’t count the energy expended in the setup process as part of the actual cost of the thing; otherwise, the enormous energy inefficiency of the narrative system will make itself very loudly known. But it does function, and it functions in a way I approve of, and I’m okay bringing it into my world.

It just seems more fair to life to set things up in a way that the dice will probably fall the way I want rather than to try and force it.

As soon as you start forcing actions, then maybe you even get exactly what you wanted, but then you’re locked into your path. Then you’ve created a system of rigidity, and the only way forward is to continue forcing actions, which in any sort of long term is unsustainable.

If you just shape your environment, you get mostly where you’re going by the end of it anyway. But eventually you start getting momentum, and the shaping gets easier, and more actions start falling into the path.

This can apply to personal relationships. This can apply to habit building. This can apply to the “fake it ’til you make it” mantra of achieving success.

It probably just works.
